English and Literature


Our school prides itself in its writers, with generations of great authors and poets have all studied in our walls. This part of the site aims to showcase one of our famous poem collections, known far and wide as: 'The Chronicles of Love and Loss.' This collection focuses on the feelings felt in a relationship (love) then, hits you with how it feels after (loss). There are voice recordings attached for certain poems.

My Love are you really mine?

My dearest, are you truly mine, all mine?
Under the moon, hole of light up above,
Would you see me, dear, in the dark thereof?
Would you find me, a grape in a grapevine?
O’ Moon, have you witnessed a love so fine?

Looking at you, your soul from far above,
My heart feels free, peaceful like a dove,
Flying star to star, feeling love divine.
Under moonlit shadows, your sweet embrace

Feels everlasting even after death,
Love, you showed me what my heart was worth.
As we traverse west, into the vast space,
I declare, with every bit of my breath
My love you are mine, unbounded by Earth.
-Jaedon Taruc

Blue Horizons

Monday, dreadful moon, I found myself on
The street, staring at the everblue sky.
Tuesday, all my friends footsteps taunt my lawn;
“Where is she?”, they clamor, “Will she come by?”

Wednesday, a lawn chair and a heart-shaped box,
Your father has a grudge; just a notion.
Thursday with the view of the stretching docs,
Our distance feels as far as the ocean.

Friday, I don’t want to make a big scene,
On the street I went, and had a moment.
Saturday, I hear echoes through concrete,
My sadness had become all apparent.

And on that day I consoled my mother,
“If there’s another, love them like you did her.”
-Jaedon Taruc
